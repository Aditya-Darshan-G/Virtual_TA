Module: Data Preparation Q3: Handling Missing Data In a dataset, you notice that several entries in the “Age” column are missing. Which method is generally not appropriate for handling these missing values? A. Replacing missing values with the mean age. B. Deleting rows with missing age values. C. Replacing missing values with a fixed age, such as 0. D. Leaving the missing values as they are without any action. Answer : D — Leaving missing values unaddressed can lead to errors in analysis and modeling. Options A, B, and C are common strategies for handling missing data, depending on the context and the extent of the missingness.
